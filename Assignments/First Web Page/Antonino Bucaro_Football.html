NFL Football ,In United States of America.

The National Football League is a professional American football league.The league contains 32 teams with a maximum of 53 players that play different postions. The NFL runs a 17-week regular season from the week after Labor Day to the week after Christmas, with each team playing sixteen games. Out of 32 teams, six from each conference compete in the NFL playoffs. All teams work hard to play for the Super Bowl but only one team is left standing. The champion's of the Super Bowl are also awarded with the Vince Lombardi Trophy. Other awards are given out to some of the players and coaches that performed their best through the season. Most games are played on Sunday afternoons but some games are also played on Mondays and Thursdays during the regular season.

The NFL was formed on August 20, 1920, as the American Professional Football Conference. The league changed its name to the American Professional Football Association on September 17, 1920. It changed its name again on June 24, 1922 to the National Football League. The first Super Bowl was held on January 1967. Today, the NFL has the highest average attendance (67,591) of any professional sports league in the world and it is the most popular sports league in the United States.The Super Bowl is among the biggest club sporting events in the world and individual Super Bowl games account for many of the most watched television programs in American history.

The World of Philosophy

A Background on Philosophy

Philosophy is a subject that has evolved and changed over the past two millennia. It is an extremely intellectually-challenging subject. One that not only demands wits, but also forces those involved to use creativity and countless hours of reading and innovating in order to come to personal conclusions on certain subjects. It began as the sole way of de-constructing and understanding reality. Aristotle is the father of all western philosophy and laid the foundation for which it grew. Others then came along to add their own statements and refute those made before them. Philosophy has made many advancements since the time of the Greeks, but with the rise of science, it is becoming disregarded in the eyes of society. People have been quick to criticize and discriminate philosophy for its apparent "lack of evidence" on subjects of truth even though science itself was first a subdivision of philosophy.

However, the truth is: Science deals with tangible truths (empiricism) whereas philosophy deals with untangible truths (rationalism). There has long been a debate between the two sides, however it should be accepted that both are necessary to achieve objective truth as they both can derive at it in their respective ways and fields. That being said, let's take a closer look at philosophy itself. As philosophy is such a broad and extremely complicated topic, let's take a look at the basics instead of jumping into the many laws and ideas that surround it.

Subsections of Philosophy

Philosophy is divided into five categories:

Categories
  Epistemology Aesthetics Metaphysics Logic Ethics
Defintion The study of knowledge (trying to decipher the difference between beliefs and truth). The study of beauty (what makes something fun, beautiful, tasteful, etc.). The study of existence (what is real, difference between the mind and body, etc.). The study of correct, rational reasoning (trying to decipher foundational, indisputable ways of figuring out truths). The study of morality (what humans should and shouldn't do, what makes something right or wrong).
Related Branches/Topics Epistemic Scepticism, Cartesian Dualism and Solipsism. Perception and Emotional values. Realism and Idealism. Mathematical Logic, Inductive Reasoning and Deductive Reasoning. Consequentialism, Categorical Imperative and Marxism.

Some of the Most Famous and Influential Philosophers:

  • Aristotle
  • Immanuel Kant
  • David Hume
  • Rene Descartes
  • Friedrich Nietzsche
  • Socrates
  • Plato
  • Thomas Aquinas
  • John Locke
  • Thomas Hobbes
  • John Stuart Mill

Though many more philosophers could have been in the list above, those particular philosophers changed the scope of philosophy to a great extent and helped make it what it is today. They wrote numerous books and texts to express their views while revolutionizing the way people looked at the world. They created basis' from which entire philosophical branches were created. For example, Descartes' "I think therefore I am" has been the subject of multiple debates in the field of epistemology. Other ideas were the foundations behind great movements, such as John Locke's Liberalism which was used as the concept behind the United States of America's Declaration of Independence. Their ideas may have been controversial, Nietzsche's "God is Dead" notion is a great example, but they helped humanity open its mind to different views. Even though they may have contradicted one another (Hume and Kant were constantly trying to prove each other wrong), their deep contemplations helped develop philosophical views, such as Mill with Utilitarianism and Nietzsche with Nihilism.
